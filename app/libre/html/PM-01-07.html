HALLUCINATION

The shadow across the hedge
is just a light dressed in black.

To stop feeling lonely,
break a mirror.

Bells toll
when her clitoris is clutched.

Simplicity becomes clothed
with the flower of silence.

Use the magic needle
to embroider the eye on your tears.

You hammer your fingers into the music
and swallows appear.

Life has its own back pain:
Heal yourself with guitars,
with teenagers' blades
or with the echo of a saint.

In the mirror
you view your face from the other side

If death is a thread,
you may weave your own hieroglyph.

The devil spewing sparks
is a brilliant idea.

The illusion is a cobweb
which never ends.

Sorrows turn to froth
in the oceans.
